About Future Airports Summit

“Developing Smart Self Sustainable Airports of the Future”

Saudi Arabia plans to increase the number of international aviation routes from 99 to over 250 and more than triple the total annual passenger traffic from 103 million in 2019 to 330 million by 2030.

Saudi Arabia will invest over $133.34 billion in airports, sea ports, rail and other infrastructure by the end of the decade in a bid to make the kingdom a global transportation and logistics hub,

As part of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia’s Vision 2030’s Economic Diversification Program the nation is looking at developing intelligent airports that are self-sustainable and contribute to the nation’s economy significantly by 2030.

In an effort to support the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia’s Vision to develop smart and sustainable airports we at Emerging Markets Middle East will be hosting a very exclusive one of its kind event known as the Future Airports Summit & Expo on the 16th & 17th of March 2022.

The Future Airports Summit & Expo will bring together international experts, key government and private aviation sector stakeholders, technology providers, buyers, influencers and investors on one  virtual platform to meet, network, share their views and  perspectives in order to move the nation towards being a global  leader in the development of smart sustainable airports. This event will feature some very  exclusive case study presentations, technology presentations, technical workshops, panel discussions, business meetings and  networking opportunities.

WHY SAUDI ARABIA?

The aviation sector is a major economic driver that contributes to the nation's economy. The sector focuses on achieving sustainable development and plays a vital role in achieving the Kingdom’s Vision 2030. The General Authority of Civil Aviation in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia is focusing strongly on developing the aviation sector and enhancing the Kingdom’s position globally as an influential country in the international civil aviation sector.

As a key regional leader in the development of next generation  solutions and technology, the country presents lucrative investment opportunities for international investors. Technologies such as 5G Mobile Networks, BIM, 3D Printing, BAS, ITS, Smart Energy Management Systems, Smart Metering, Renewable Energy, Fintech, Artificial intelligence Virtual Reality, Robotics, Autonomous Vehicles and Drones, are being integrated into various development projects which is why Saudi Arabia is investing billions on modernizing the existing airports as well as developing new smart sustainable airports of the future to increase passenger flow and to improve passenger experience.

Dr. Konrad Pesendorfer

President

General Authority for Statistics

Kingdom of Saudi Arabia

Dr. Konrad Pesendorfer is President of the General Authority for Statistics (GASTAT) of the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. He holds this position since the beginning of 2020. Before that, he was Director General of Statistics Austria for ten years, being responsible for all statistical matters and international affairs. He represented Austria in the European Statistical System Committee (ESSC), the top decision making body of the European Statistical System. Mr. Pesendorfer was chairman of the OECD’s Committee on Statistics and Statistical Policy between 2016 and 2019, he was Co-Chair (together with India) of the International Comparison Program Governing Board of the UN/Worldbank, set up to calculate worldwide Purchasing Power Parities. He was a member of the Austrian Fiscal Council, assessing and advising the government on fiscal policy issues, as well as President of the Austrian Statistical Society between 2017 and 2019. Mr. Pesendorfer started his professional career as an economist at the Oesterreichische Nationalbank, the Austrian Central Bank, and at the Federal Ministry of Finance, was Economic and Financial Counsellor at the Permanent Mission of Austria to the OECD and Counsellor to the Executive Board of the European Central Bank in Frankfurt. From 2008 to 2009, Mr. Pesendorfer was the Economic Policy Adviser to the Austrian Prime Minister. Mr. Pesendorfer studied economics at the Vienna University of Economics and Business Administration and holds a Ph.D. in economics. He was lecturer on international economics at the H.E.C. Business School in Paris and at the Institut d’Etudes Politiques de Paris (Sciences Po) in Paris.

Mohamed Abdel-Aal is a founding member of King Abdullah University of Science & Technology (KAUST) where he joined the institution during its development and construction phase in 2008. Mohamed had previously managed the University’s IT infrastructure and research computing including systems, servers, storage, data center facilities, HPC clusters, and an extensive network that spans the academic buildings and labs, and the community and its housing facilities. Mohamed also oversees strategic digital transformation plans and initiatives including partnerships and collaborations with leading industry players to utilize KAUST’s unique setting as a launch pad for pilots and innovative creative concepts and new ideas. Mohamed holds an MBA and a MS degree from Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute in Troy, New York, and a BS degree from King Fahd University of Petroleum and Minerals (KFUPM).
